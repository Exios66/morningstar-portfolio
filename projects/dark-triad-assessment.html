Dark Triad Inventory Assessments

The Dark Triad Inventory includes a comprehensive set of assessments designed to evaluate key personality traits associated with the Dark Triad: Machiavellianism, Narcissism, and Psychopathy. Below is a list of the assessments included:

  • Dirty Dozen

    The Dirty Dozen is a concise 12-item questionnaire that measures the Dark Triad traits, providing a quick overview of an individual's tendencies toward Narcissism, Machiavellianism, and Psychopathy.

  • Narcissistic Personality Inventory (NPI)

    The NPI is a widely used assessment for measuring subclinical Narcissism, capturing both grandiose and entitlement-based behaviors and tendencies.

  • The MACH-IV

    The MACH-IV is an established inventory used to assess the level of Machiavellianism in individuals, focusing on manipulative behavior, lack of morality, and pragmatic thinking.

  • SDT3

    The Short Dark Triad 3 (SDT3) is a brief assessment that measures the three Dark Triad traits using a series of targeted questions aimed at revealing the core characteristics of each trait.

  • SDT4

    The Short Dark Triad 4 (SDT4) is an updated version of SDT3, including additional questions for greater accuracy and depth in measuring Machiavellianism, Narcissism, Psychopathy, and a newly included facet that evaluates secondary psychopathy traits.

  • OCEAN Model (Big Five Personality Traits)

    The OCEAN model, also known as the Big Five, is included to provide a broader perspective on an individual's personality, helping to contextualize Dark Triad traits alongside Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, and Neuroticism.

  • HEXACO Model

    The HEXACO personality model is used for an in-depth examination of personality traits, including Honesty-Humility, which has been linked to tendencies opposite of the Dark Triad characteristics.

  • Synthesized MMPI Assessment

    A synthesized assessment drawing from the Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory (MMPI-2 & MMPI-3) is included to provide a detailed psychological evaluation. This synthesized assessment combines elements from both versions to explore personality patterns and potential psychopathologies relevant to Dark Triad traits.

Background and History of the Assessments

Each of the Dark Triad assessments has a unique history and foundation that contributes to our understanding of personality traits. Below is an overview of the background for each assessment:

  • Dirty Dozen

    The Dirty Dozen was developed by Jonason and Webster in 2010 as a shorter, more efficient tool for measuring the Dark Triad traits compared to longer inventories. It aimed to provide a balance between brevity and accuracy in assessing Narcissism, Machiavellianism, and Psychopathy.

  • Narcissistic Personality Inventory (NPI)

    The Narcissistic Personality Inventory was developed by Raskin and Hall in 1979. It was one of the first tools designed specifically for assessing non-clinical, or subclinical, narcissism. The NPI draws inspiration from the DSM criteria for Narcissistic Personality Disorder but is intended for general population research.

  • The MACH-IV

    The MACH-IV was developed by Richard Christie and Florence Geis in the 1970s. It is based on the writings of Niccol√≤ Machiavelli and measures manipulative tendencies, cynical worldview, and a lack of morality. The MACH-IV has been influential in the study of personality and social behavior.

  • SDT3 and SDT4

    The Short Dark Triad (SDT3) was introduced by Jones and Paulhus in 2014 as a brief but effective measure of the Dark Triad traits. The SDT4, an updated version of SDT3, was later developed to improve accuracy and add more nuance to the measurement of psychopathy, including secondary psychopathy traits.

  • OCEAN Model (Big Five Personality Traits)

    The OCEAN model, or Big Five, has its roots in the work of several researchers, including Lewis Goldberg and Costa & McCrae in the 1980s. It was developed to provide a comprehensive overview of personality traits, and its inclusion in Dark Triad research helps contextualize these traits within broader personality dimensions.

    The Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory (MMPI) was originally developed in the late 1930s and early 1940s by Hathaway and McKinley. The MMPI-2 and MMPI-3 are updated versions that provide insights into psychopathological behaviors. The synthesized assessment used here combines elements from both versions to evaluate personality traits linked to the Dark Triad.
